Master thesis: GNC of a sounding rocket

EPFL Rocket Team : Dec. 2020 - July. 2021

My master thesis was part of project Icarus (see this page) and supervised by Professor Colin Jones and Dr. Peter Listov of the Automatic Control Laboratory at EPFL.
Besides managing the project, I had to work on new guidance, navigation, and control (GNC) algorithms for our student-developed rockets.
I developed two Model Predictive Control algorithms: one for attitude and position tracking, and the other one for optimal trajectory planning.

I realized the need for a common framework for better cooperation between the student projects, so I developed the open-source real_time_simulator project based on ROS, which defines a modular software and hardware architecture, an interface to a custom real-time rocket simulator, and to our test bench for Hardware-in-the-Loop tests.
This framework was used by two major projects last year. The first one was for a custom drone we built as a test platform for our algorithms. Most of these algorithms were developed by RaphaÃ«l Linsen, then tested with the team. You can find more information on our recent paper to ICRA.
The second project was an altitude controller for our Bellalui 2 rocket flying at the 2021 EuRoc competition. The goal was to reach a precise altitude at apogee by controlling the shutdown time of our engine to reach the apogee ballistically. I used my MPC-based trajectory planner to predict the apogee and a continuous-time Kalman filter to estimate the rocket state. As we only have one chance to launch the rocket, the biggest challenge for me was to test the GNC reliably on the ground. Using my simulator, we could do software and processor-in-the-loop tests, so the final GNC was completely nominal during the launch and our team won the European competition!
I am continuing to develop this framework as of today, as a new generation of student is continuing our work and need support to develop new features. One of our objective is to finish the development of the test bench and its integration with the simulator to do Hardware-in-the-Loop simulation.
