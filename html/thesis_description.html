Master thesis: GNC of a sounding rocket

EPFL Rocket Team : Dec. 2020 - July. 2021

My master thesis was part of project Icarus, and supervised by professor Colin Jones and Dr. Peter Listov of the Automatic Control Laboratory at EPFL.
Besides my management responsibilities, I had to work on new guidance, navigation and control (GNC) algorithms for our student developed rockets.
I developed two Model Predictive Control algorithms: one attitude and position tracking, and the other one for optimal trajectory planning.

I realized the need for a common framework for better cooperation between the students projects, so I developed the open-source real_time_simulator project based on ROS, which defines a modular software and hardware architecture, an interface to a custom real-time rocket simulator, and to our test bench for Hardware-in-the-Loop tests.
This framework was used on two major project last year. The first one was for a custom drone we built as a test plateform for our algorithms. Most of these algorithms were developed by RaphaÃ«l Linsen, then tested with the team. You can find more information on our recent paper to ICRA.
The second project was an altitude controller meant to be deployed on our Bellalui 2 rocket for the EuRoc competition. The goal was to reach a precise altitude at apogee by controlling the shutdwn time of our engine to reach the apogee balistically. I used my MPC-based trajectory planner to predict the apogee and a continuous-time kalman filter to estimate the rocket state. As we only have one chance to launch the rocket, the biggest challenge for me was to test the GNC reliably on th ground. Using my simulator, we could do software and processor in-the loop tests, so the final GNC was completely nominal during the launch and our team won the European competition !
