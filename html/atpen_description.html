Project ATPen: Developemnt of an anti-tremor pen

Debiopharm Challenge: Aug. 2021 - Nov. 2021

This is a small project I did with my friend Yves Martin. The objective is to develop a special pen to help people with essential tremor writing better. This idea was selected as part of the Debiopharm challenge, and financed 5k$ to create a first prototype in four months.





For my third year at the EPFL Rocket Team, I started a new research project to replace our passive rocket fins stabilization with thrust vector control (TVC), thus allowing to control the rocket trajectory and explore propulsive landing technologies.

After a semester of research in Autumn 2020, I recruited a team of 12 students to work on the TVC mechanism and test bench, and help me develop the Guidance, Navigation and Control (GNC) algorithms I was working on as part of my thesis.
To control the thrust direction of the rocket engine, we developed custom jet vanes, which are small aerodynmaic surfaces that redirects the flow of the engine. We simulated, manufactured and tested many different shapes and materials to have the right thermal and aerodynamic properties.

A custom PCB was developed by Iacopo Sprenger to integrate a RaspberryPi 4 into our modular flight computer. This gives us a lot more processing power and let us experiment with advanced GNC algorithms.
To control the vehicle, we used Model Predictive Control (MPC) running on the Rapsberry Pi at 50Hz to directly control the actuators. Another MPC was used at lower frequency to generate optimal reference trajectories for the controller. Finally we used an extended Kalman filter to estimates external perturbations and model mismatches.
