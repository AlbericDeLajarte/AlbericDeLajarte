Project ATPen: Developemnt of an anti-tremor pen

Debiopharm Challenge: Aug. 2021 - Nov. 2021

This is a small project I did with my friend Yves Martin. The objective was to develop a special pen to help people with essential tremor writing better. This idea was selected as part of the Debiopharm challenge, and financed 5'000$ to create a fonctional prototype in four months. We had to compete with five other teams working on other projects related to health technologies.

Our plan was to develop a damping system that would absorb the tremor vibration around 5Hz. To make our design simpler, cheaper and more robust, we opted for a fully mechanical solution based on spring damper technologies. The tip of the pen can be decoupled from the pen handle by integrating a custom spring in between. Then the cut-off frequency can be tuned by weighting the pen and adding damping elements.
The difficulty was to integrate all these elements in a pen, while guaranteeing good damping performances. After many iterations, we opted for a magnetic damping based on Eddy's current that would use very little space. We manufactured this design using conventional machining and 3D printing. After some satisfactory tests with a patient, we presented our results to the jury and won the second place and a special technical price for the quality of the design.
This price allowed us to finance a new chapter for this project: we started a more rigorous study of our prototype to see how patients used our device and measure it's impact on their writting. After analyzing our first results, we are considering to develop a new version based on active damping with closed-loop control in order to improve the damping performances.
